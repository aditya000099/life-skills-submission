# Listening and Active Communication

### 1. What are the steps / strategies to do Active Listening?

1. Give full attention to the speaker. Do not think about your reply while they are talking.
2. Do not interrupt. Let the person complete their point.
3. Use body language like eye contact and nodding to show you are listening.
4. Ask questions if something is not clear.
5. Paraphrase what the speaker said to confirm understanding.
6. Take notes during important discussions.

### 2. According to Fisher’s model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

1. Listen carefully to both words and emotions.
2. Restate the message in your own words.
3. Confirm with the speaker if your understanding is correct.
4. Stay neutral and avoid judging or advising immediately.
5. Focus on clarity to reduce misunderstandings.

### 3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?

1. Thinking about my response instead of listening fully.
2. Distractions like phone or notifications.
3. Tiredness or low energy.
4. Assuming I already know what the speaker will say.
5. Difficulty understanding accents or fast speech.

### 4. What can you do to improve your listening?

1. Be fully present in conversations.
2. Keep the phone away during discussions.
3. Ask questions instead of guessing.
4. Take notes in meetings.
5. Slow down and listen without rushing to reply.

### 5. When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?

1. When talking to seniors or authority figures.
2. When I want to avoid conflict.
3. When I am not confident about the topic.
4. In new or unfamiliar environments.
5. When the issue feels minor.

### 6. When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

1. When I feel disrespected.
2. When I am stressed or angry.
3. When the same issue happens repeatedly.
4. When I feel ignored.
5. During heated arguments.

### 7. When do you switch into Passive-Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

1. When I am upset but avoid direct confrontation.
2. When I feel hurt or ignored.
3. When I use sarcasm instead of speaking clearly.
4. When I stay silent to show dissatisfaction.
5. When I feel uncomfortable expressing feelings openly.

### 8. How can you make your communication assertive?

1. Speak clearly and honestly.
2. Use a calm and respectful tone.
3. Listen before responding.
4. Use “I” statements instead of blaming others.
5. Set boundaries politely.
6. Control emotions before speaking.
7. Be confident without being rude.
