# Energy Management Reflection


### Question 1: Activities that make me relax

- Listening to music.
- Sitting quietly and watching my breath for a few minutes.
- Spending time with family or friends.
- Writing down things going in my mind on a paper.
- Believe consciously that I'm just overthinking and pause my thinking for a few seconds.
- Quietly observe people around me that are enjoying their lives according to my perspective

### Question 2: When I enter the stress quadrant

- When I have deadlines approaching or multiple tasks at the same time.
- Before exams or important evaluations.
- When I overthink about the future.
- When I delay important work

### Question 3: How I know I am in the excitement quadrant

- I feel naturally motivated to work.
- I feel curious to learn more.
- I feel confident, focused and attention increases.
- I feel urge to do more in less time
- Time feels like passing very quickly.

### Question 4: Sleep is your superpower

- Sleep improves learning and memory retention.
- Sleep regulates emotions and reduces stress.
- Sleep improves focus and productivity.
- Deep sleep helps the body repair and recover.
- Poor sleep affects mental health.
- Sleep deprivation reduces lifespan.
- Consistently following a sleep schedule improves overall performance.

### Question 5: Ideas I can implement to sleep better

- Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day.
- Avoid screens at least 30 minutes before sleeping.
- Do meditation before bed.
- Avoid heavy meals right before sleeping.
- Sit under sunlight during the day.
- Exercise regularly to make my body tired.

### Question 6: Brain changing benefits of exercise

- It increases focus and attention span.
- Physical activity reduces stress hormones.
- It enhances memory and learning ability.
- Exercise helps keeps eyes healthy.
- It improves sleep quality.
- It increases energy levels throughout the day.

### Question 7: Steps I can take to exercise more

- Choose activities I actually enjoy.
- Track progress to stay consistent.
- Use short breaks during work.
- Start with short exercise sessions.
- Add a mix of easy and hard exercises so I don't feel it like a hard task
- Play my favorite sport (badminton) at alternate days.
- Set realistic weekly goals instead of extreme ones.
